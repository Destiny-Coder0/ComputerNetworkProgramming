Plant Information

Violet

Violets are popular indoor flowering plants known for their vibrant purple, blue, or white petals. They prefer indirect sunlight and moderate watering. Violets bloom year-round if kept in the right conditions, and they symbolize modesty and faithfulness.

Cactus

A desert plant that is unique and hardy plants that thrive in dry, hot environments. They store water in their thick stems, making them perfect for people who might forget to water regularly. They can be in many different shapes and sizes. They love bright sunlight and require very little attention, which makes them an ideal choice for beginners or anyone who wants a low-maintenance plant.

Tulip

Tulips are classic spring-blooming flowers that come in a rainbow of colors. They are often associated with love, rebirth, and elegance. Tulips grow from bulbs and need to be planted in the fall to bloom beautifully in spring gardens.

Photosynthesis
The process by which green plants use sunlight to synthesize foods from carbon dioxide and water.
Soil pH
Refers to the acidity or alkalinity of the soil. A balanced pH level is essential for plant health.
Indirect Sunlight
Light that does not hit the plant directly. Ideal for indoor plants like Violets or Rubber Plants.
Watering Schedule
How often a plant needs to be watered. Overwatering is a common problem!
USDA Zone
A geographic zone that defines which plants grow best in that area based on climate.
